CURRENTLY IN-USE PRACTICES EFFICIENT PRACTICES ADOPTED BY US ADVANTAGES & BENEFITS
Randomly deciding depth of recharge bore well and slotted portion of casing pipes. Geo-electric study to determine type of strata and water level likely to be encountered at various depths below proposed location of recharge bore well. Prior knowledge of type of strata helps in deciding correct depth of recharge bore well and placing slotted pipes at right depths which ensures proper recharge in good aquifers and less chances of clogging of slots (which occurs when slotted pipes are placed in clayey strata).
No attention paid to estimating intake capacity of aquifer in which recharge is planned. Recharge test in existing bore well helps in reaching a good estimation of intake capacity of its surrounding aquifer. Correct estimation of design and capacity of recharge structures.
PVC SWR pipes with no adherence to standards are used as casing pipes in recharge bore well for all depths. ISI pipes adhering to standards with suitable specifications like shallow well (CS), medium well (CM) or suitable pressure rating are preferred for different depths. Prevention of pipe collapse due to surrounding pressure of gravels.
Crusher gravel or river gravel with no proper grading and washing is used as gravel pack in recharge bore. Properly graded and washed gravel with rounded ends and of standard size (3-8mm) is used as gravel pack in recharge bore. Less chances of clogging, good development of bore well and good percolation rate.
Casing pipes in recharge pit are slotted manually to allow entry of rainwater into recharge bore well. V-Wire screen is fitted with casing pipe in recharge pit.
  1. Continuous slots in V- Wire screen provide more than 8 times % open area as in manually slotted PVC/MS screens and 5 times % open area as in company-slotted ISI PVC/MS screens for a given screen diameter and slot size. Recharge rate through V-wire screen is at least double as compared to other PVC/MS slotted pipes for a given screen diameter and slot size.

  1. Inwardly widening V- shaped slots provide non-clogging properties to V-wire screen as compared to regular slotted screens which are highly susceptible to clogging. It gives longer effective life to recharge well and sustained recharge process.
  2. Made from Stainless Steel which is anti- corrosive. Gives long life to recharge bore well.
  1. Slots in V-wire screen are arranged horizontal to the axis of pipes. This offers superior flow properties as compared to screen with vertical slots. Water has to change direction to enter a normal vertically slotted pipe and this affects the free flow of water into the well. On the other hand horizontal slots in V- wire screen achieve high permeability along with low turbulence.
Recharge pits are constructed either with less dia. RCC rings or as shallow brick masonry structures. Recharge pit is constructed in cylindrical shape in RCC cast at site. When constructing recharge pit with RCC rings following points are kept in mind:
  • At least 1220mm diameter
  • At least 50mm wall thickness
  • Height to be 600mm
  • Cylindrical pit provides better structural strength than a rectangular one.
  • Entire construction can be done safely and till much greater depths.
  • Due to high head of water in such a structure, recharge process is also better.





  • Sufficient diameter of rings allow worker to move freely while carrying out work of fitting V-wire screen and laying filter media.
  • Proper wall thickness imparts strength to rings. Excavation can be done in reverse manner to prevent any accidents. Also there is less damage during transportation.
  • Proper height leads to less no. of joints, so less chances of caving in of soil and less installation time.
Large desilting pit is constructed before recharge pit to allow sedimentation of impurities in rainwater.

In case of large roofs desilting pit with sieve bend V-wire screen is provided to remove most of the impurities in rainwater before entering recharge pit.

In case of smaller roofs, desilting pit is replaced with PRECIPURE TM - a unique filtration system having first flush arrangement and V-Wire screen. This system removes most of the impurities in rainwater before entering recharge pit.

 		In case of conventional de-silting pits, proper sedimentation is hard to achieve due to continuous inflow of rainwater which results in dissolved impurities entering recharge pit. Preventing maximum impurities coming with first rains and with rains after long gaps from entering the recharge pit is critical in successful functioning of rainwater harvesting system. This can be achieved by providing desilting pit with sieve bend V-Wire screen or PRECIPURE TM filtration system. These are relatively much smaller in size thus occupy less space and less costly than conventional desilting pit.
Filter media used in recharge pit is crusher gravel with pointed ends or round gravel and sand which is neither graded nor washed.
Different layers of filter media are directly put over each other with no emphasis on minimum thickness of each layer. 		Gravel used as filter media in recharge pit should be round, properly graded and washed. Sand used as filter media should be free of dirt (or preferably washed) and properly graded. Different layers of filter media should be properly separated by nylon/PVC/ fibre mesh. Minimum thickness of each layer of filter media should be 0.3m. Proper filtration and non-clogging properties are achieved.
Since intermixing of filter material is not allowed, the efficiency of filter improves as also the ease in maintenance.
Not enough attention is paid on providing ventilation in rainwater harvesting structures. Proper ventilation of desilting pit, recharge pit and recharge bore well is done by making provision for air pipes. Air is not trapped in the recharge bore well and pits during movement of water and thus proper flow of water is maintained
Due emphasis not given on development of recharge bore well before final commissioning of rainwater harvesting system. Development of recharge bore well by bailing machine is done before final commissioning of rainwater harvesting system. Ensures removal of drilling mud, silt and any foreign matter which might have entered recharge bore well during drilling process and construction of recharge pit. This process must be undertaken mandatorily so as to achieve good recharge rate of recharge bore well.
